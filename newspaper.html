Embattled Australian cricketers Steve Smith, David Warner and Cameron Bancroft have been given the final sanctions by Cricket Australia CEO James Sutherland, and the trio are set to be out of cricketing action for quite a lengthy time. While Warner and Smith have been handed a 1 year ban, Bancroft will be out for 9 months. The ban also prevents them from playing in the Indian Premier League, meaning their franchises will have to find appropriate replacements in such a short period of time.

Sutherland, in his press conference on Tuesday, had said that the final punishments for the trio will justify the depth of their acts and before the decision was made official, reports of Smith and Warner being given a 12-month ban had surfaced.

Vice-captain Warner, along with Australian skipper Steve Smith and opener Cameron Bancroft, are in the line of fire due to the scandal that broke out during the third Test against South Africa in Cape Town. Bancroft was caught on camera while using a yellow tape to tamper the ball.
